
# Quantitative Finance Course

Welcome to the Quantitative Finance Course repository!

This course is designed to provide a clear, intuitive, and practical introduction to the core mathematical concepts and pricing techniques used in quantitative finance. Each module is based on a focused presentation, with an emphasis on building intuition and practical understanding rather than rigorous proofs.

## Course Modules

### 1. Mathematics for Derivatives Pricing
**PDF:** Mathematics for Derivatives Pricing.pdf

This module offers the most complete introduction to the basic mathematics of derivatives pricing. It is ideal for anyone starting in the field and feeling overwhelmed by the breadth of required mathematical knowledge. The focus is on intuition and practical applications, equipping you with the tools to begin building your own pricing models.

**Topics include:**
- Probability foundations for pricing under uncertainty
- Expected value, variance, and Jensen’s inequality
- Convexity intuition and its role in payoff valuation
- Core calculus ideas behind Greeks and no-arbitrage conditions
- The PDE view vs. risk-neutral expectation view (and their connection)
- Stochastic calculus basics (GBM, SDEs, Feynman-Kac)
- Numerical methods (Monte Carlo, finite differences)
- Linear algebra essentials (including Cholesky decomposition)

### 2. Stochastic Calculus
**PDF:** Stochastic Calculus.pdf

This module is your guide to learning stochastic calculus, starting from the basics and progressing to advanced topics. It addresses foundational questions such as the differentiability of Brownian motion, quadratic variation, and the application of Itô’s Lemma to solve stochastic differential equations. The explanations are clear and intuitive, with simple proofs where appropriate.

**Topics include:**
1. Random Walk
2. Brownian Motion
3. Functions of Brownian Motion
4. Itô’s Lemma

### 3. Risk-Neutral Pricing
**PDF:** Risk Neutral Pricing.pdf

This module introduces the core ideas of risk-neutral pricing (RNP) in both discrete-time and continuous-time frameworks. It explains how to determine the fair price of a derivative, the uniqueness of this price, and the behavior of the underlying asset. The approach is hands-on, starting with the binomial model and progressing to continuous-time models using stochastic calculus and the Feynman–Kac theorem.

**Topics include:**
- Discrete-time pricing with the binomial model
- Replicating portfolios and fair pricing
- Transition to continuous-time models
- Stochastic calculus and the Feynman–Kac theorem
